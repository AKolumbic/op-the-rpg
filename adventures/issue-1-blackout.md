# OP the RPG — Issue #1: Blackout

> A one-shot adventure for 3-5 players, levels 1-3. Set in Paramount City. Expandable to 2-3 sessions.

---

## Adventure Overview

**Logline:** On an ordinary Tuesday night, half of Paramount City loses power. In the darkness, three coordinated attacks unfold — a tower bombing, a convoy ambush, and a hospital siege — all orchestrated by a private military contractor called Bastion Group to cover up illegal experiments on powered individuals. The players are the only ones who show up.

**Tone:** Adult superhero. Grounded consequences, real stakes, dark humor where it fits. Think Dispatch, think early Daredevil, think the first hour of a comic book crossover event — the one where the heroes don't know each other yet and everything is going wrong at once.

**Structure:** Sandbox. Three incidents happen simultaneously. The players start at different locations, investigate in any order, and converge as the threads connect. The adventure climaxes at The Barrens, where Bastion is extracting the one person who can expose the whole operation.

**Runtime:** 3-4 hours as a tight one-shot. Can expand to 2-3 sessions if players investigate thoroughly or if the GM wants to develop NPC relationships and the Bastion conspiracy.

---

## Paramount City

The biggest city in the country. A skyline that doesn't quit, a university district that produces more patents than most nations, a financial core that moves global capital, and neighborhoods where the streetlights have been broken since before you were born. Paramount City is where ambition comes to succeed or die. Twelve million people, and on any given night, most of them are trying not to think about what's happening on the next block.

### Districts

**The Spire District**
The financial heart. Glass towers, corporate headquarters, private security on every corner. Kendrick-Voss Biotech has its regional HQ here — a 40-story building with a restricted-access research floor on 31. The streets are wide, well-lit (when the power's on), and nearly empty after 8 PM. The people who work here don't live here.

**Ridgeline University**
The country's premier research institution. Sprawling campus on the city's north side — old stone buildings mixed with brutalist concrete labs from the '60s and modern glass additions. The biomedical sciences building is where Dr. Samir Osei works late most nights. The campus has its own power grid, which means it stays lit during the blackout — a potential safe harbor or staging ground for the players.

**Mercy General Hospital**
The biggest public hospital in Paramount City. Perpetually overcrowded, perpetually underfunded. Located in Midtown, it serves everyone the private hospitals won't take. The ER on a Tuesday night is already a controlled disaster — gunshots, overdoses, car accidents. When the power cuts and armed men walk in, it becomes something else entirely.

**Midtown**
Dense residential and commercial. Apartments, bodegas, diners, laundromats, walk-ups. This is where normal people live. The Crosstown Freeway runs through it — an elevated highway that's always congested. The convoy ambush happens on the Midtown stretch of the Crosstown, shutting down traffic in both directions and creating a chaos zone of abandoned cars and panicking commuters.

**The Barrens**
Industrial waterfront on the city's south end. Abandoned shipping infrastructure, rusted cranes, empty container yards, and warehouses that haven't been inspected since the '70s. Bastion Group is using Warehouse 11 — a decommissioned cold storage facility — as their extraction point. A helicopter is inbound. This is where the adventure ends.

### The Power Grid

Paramount City's electrical grid is divided into six sectors. The blackout hits Sectors 2, 3, and 4 — covering The Spire District, Midtown, and the northern edge of The Barrens. Sectors 1 (including Ridgeline University), 5, and 6 remain lit. This is not a coincidence. Bastion's tech team triggered the blackout via a cyberattack on the Paramount City Power Authority's control systems. The blackout is designed to last exactly 90 minutes — long enough to complete all three operations and extract before emergency systems restore power.

**What the blackout means for players:**
- Cell networks overload and crash within 10 minutes. Landlines work but most people under 40 don't know a number to call.
- Traffic signals are dead. Intersections become gridlock or chaos depending on the neighborhood.
- Emergency services are overwhelmed. Police are stretched across three simultaneous crises. Fire department is responding to the tower explosion. Ambulances can't get through Midtown traffic.
- The city feels wrong. Paramount City is never dark. Tonight it is.

## The Setup

### Read-Aloud: Opening

> *It's a Tuesday. Late October. The kind of night where the city smells like wet concrete and exhaust. Nothing special. Nothing worth remembering.*
>
> *You're doing what you do on a Tuesday night — working, or pretending to, or trying not to think about the thing you've been trying not to think about. The city hums the way it always does. Twelve million people, all convinced their problems are the only ones that matter.*
>
> *And then the lights go out.*
>
> *Not a flicker. Not a brownout. One second the city is there — blazing, loud, alive — and the next, half the skyline drops into nothing. The hum dies. The glow dies. Car horns start immediately, then screams, then sirens. You can see the half of the city that still has power, shining in the distance like it's on the other side of a wall.*
>
> *Your phone buzzes once. Then the network crashes.*
>
> *And somewhere in the dark, something explodes.*

## Act 1: The Three Incidents

When the blackout hits, three events trigger within sixty seconds of each other. The GM should describe the blackout, then cut to each player's location and describe what happens to them specifically. This is a fast open — let each player react to their incident for a round or two, then start cutting between groups.

### Incident 1: The Tower (Spire District)

**What happens:** Sixty seconds after the blackout, Floor 31 of Kendrick-Voss Tower detonates. Shaped charges — clean, professional, not designed to bring the building down but to completely destroy everything on that floor. The explosion blows out windows on floors 29-33. Debris rains onto the street. The fire suppression system is offline (no power). Smoke fills the stairwells.

**Marcus is in the lobby** when the building shakes. Ceiling tiles fall. The security desk monitors go dead. A few late-working employees stumble down the stairs, coughing and panicking.

**What Marcus finds if he goes up:**
- Floors 29-30 are accessible but smoke-filled. Emergency lighting flickers.
- Floor 31 is destroyed. The stairwell door is blown off its hinges. The entire floor is gutted — lab equipment, filing cabinets, servers, all reduced to slag and rubble. This was not an accident.
- **Investigation (DC 12 Intelligence/Science or Wisdom/Perception):** The blast pattern is surgical. Shaped charges placed at structural columns and around specific rooms. Someone knew exactly what was on this floor and wanted it gone.
- **Search (DC 14 Intelligence/Investigation):** In the wreckage, Marcus finds a half-melted ID badge. Name illegible, but the department reads: **"DIVISION 9 — RESTRICTED."** Kendrick-Voss doesn't have a Division 9 on any public org chart.
- **Perception (DC 13):** Boot prints in the dust and debris. Multiple sets. Tactical tread. Someone was up here *after* the explosion — or just before it.
- On a dead Bastion operator (if Marcus engages the cleanup team — see below): an encrypted radio, a Bastion Group tactical patch (subdued, no flag), and a keycard for **Warehouse 11, Barrens Industrial Park.**

**The Bastion Cleanup Team:** A four-person team in black tactical gear is working Floor 31 when Marcus arrives (or arrives shortly after he does). They're recovering hard drives and physical files that survived the blast. They are professional, armed (suppressed pistols, one shotgun), and have orders to leave no witnesses on the restricted floor.

- If Marcus encounters them: they will first try to order him to leave ("Building security doesn't have clearance for this floor. Go back to the lobby. Now."). If he doesn't comply or asks too many questions, they engage.
- **Bastion Operator (4):** Use the **Bastion Operator** stat block.
- The team leader carries the encrypted radio. If captured alive (or if the radio is recovered), a DC 15 Intelligence/Science check can partially decrypt the chatter — references to "Mercy General," "the package," and "Ironhand confirms extraction at Warehouse 11."

**What this incident reveals:**
- Kendrick-Voss had a secret research division (Division 9).
- Someone professionally destroyed it during the blackout.
- The same organization is operating at Mercy General.
- Warehouse 11 in The Barrens is the extraction point.

---

### Incident 2: The Convoy (Crosstown Freeway)

**What happens:** An unmarked armored transport — two black SUVs flanking a windowless cargo truck — is moving through Midtown on the Crosstown Freeway when the blackout hits. Thirty seconds later, the lead SUV hits a spike strip. The rear SUV is rammed by a heavy vehicle. The convoy is boxed in.

Bastion operators emerge from pre-positioned vehicles and hit the convoy fast — flashbangs, suppressive fire, coordinated breach of the cargo truck. The whole ambush takes less than two minutes. The convoy guards (Kendrick-Voss private security, not Bastion) are outclassed.

**Dee is watching from the overpass** when the blackout changes everything. Her job was to intercept the convoy at a different point using a different method — this ambush isn't hers. Someone else is hitting the same target. She's watching professionals do what she was hired to do, and they're doing it with military hardware.

**What Dee sees/finds:**
- **Observation (no check needed):** This is a military operation. Eight or more operators, night vision, encrypted comms, precision tactics. These aren't criminals.
- **If Dee approaches the cargo truck:** The back is open. Inside: six medical transport pods — climate-controlled, with IV lines and monitoring equipment. Four pods are occupied. The occupants are sedated, dressed in hospital gowns, with **medical ID bands from Kendrick-Voss Biotech, Division 9.**
- **Investigation (DC 12):** The sedated individuals show signs of prolonged medical experimentation — injection sites, surgical scarring, unusual skin discoloration. One of them has faint electrical burns across their arms and chest. Another's veins are visibly dark, almost black.
- **Perception (DC 14):** One of the Bastion operators is speaking into a radio: *"Cargo secured. Four of six viable. Two didn't survive transport. Moving to secondary extraction. Ironhand, confirm Barrens ETA."*
- **Sleight of Hand / Stealth (DC 13):** Dee can grab a manifest from the truck cab. It lists six patient numbers (no names), a destination code (**WH-11-BRN**), and a single authorization signature: **S. Creed.**

**If Dee intervenes:** The Bastion operators at the convoy number 6. They're focused on the cargo and the surviving Kendrick-Voss guards. Dee can use the chaos to free subjects, steal evidence, sabotage vehicles, or slip away. Direct confrontation against six operators is extremely dangerous for a single character.

**Civilian chaos:** The freeway is gridlocked. Dozens of cars are stopped. Commuters are panicking, hiding behind their vehicles, or trying to flee on foot. This provides cover but also means collateral damage is a real risk.

**What this incident reveals:**
- Kendrick-Voss was conducting experiments on human subjects.
- Bastion Group is recovering those subjects — not rescuing them.
- The subjects are being taken to Warehouse 11 in The Barrens.
- The operation commander is someone named S. Creed.

---

### Incident 3: The Siege (Mercy General Hospital)

**What happens:** Three minutes after the blackout, twelve armed men enter Mercy General Hospital through the ER entrance, the loading dock, and a service corridor. They are not Bastion operators — they're hired muscle, local contractors, a mix of ex-cons and mercenaries paid very well to do a specific job: **lock down the ER floor and find a patient named Elena Vasik.**

The hired guns are loud, aggressive, and not nearly as disciplined as Bastion's own people. This is intentional. The siege is half-operation, half-diversion — it pulls emergency responders to Mercy General and away from the other two incidents.

**Jordan is in the ER waiting room** when armed men come through the front doors. Shouting, zip ties, everyone on the ground. The lights are already out. Emergency generators kick in after thirty seconds, giving the ER dim red backup lighting.

**Rosa is in the ER bay** behind the double doors when she hears the shouting. She has a three-second head start before the gunmen push through into the treatment area.

**The situation inside:**
- Approximately 40 civilians in the ER (patients, visitors, staff). The gunmen herd everyone into the main waiting area and zip-tie anyone who resists.
- The gunmen are searching patient records (paper backups — the computers are down) for Elena Vasik. She checked in under the name **"Maria Santos"** and is in **Room 4B** on the second floor, being treated for acute toxic exposure. The hired guns don't know the alias yet.
- **The leader of the hired guns is Terrence Sharpe** — a career criminal who's smart enough to know he's in over his head and dumb enough to have taken the money anyway. He's nervous, and nervous men with guns are dangerous.

**What Jordan and Rosa can do:**
- **Stealth (DC 12):** Move through the ER without being spotted. The backup lighting is dim and the gunmen are focused on the records desk and herding civilians.
- **Perception (DC 11):** Overhear Sharpe on a radio: *"We're looking. She's not on the board under that name. Creed, you sure she's here?"* A calm voice responds: *"She's there. Room-by-room if you have to. You have forty minutes."*
- **Persuasion/Intimidation (DC 14):** Convince Sharpe to stand down. He's in this for the money, not ideology. If he realizes the situation is bigger than a payday, he might fold — or he might panic.
- **Medicine (DC 10, Rosa has advantage):** Rosa can identify that one of the ER patients — an older woman in Room 4B listed as Maria Santos — is suffering from **ricin-like symptoms.** Someone poisoned her. She's dying without treatment.
- **Direct engagement:** The hired guns number 12, but they're spread across two floors and they're not coordinated. In small groups of 2-3, they're manageable fights for powered characters. In the full group, they're a problem.

**Dr. Vasik:**
Elena Vasik is 58, gaunt, terrified, and fading. She was the lead researcher on Division 9's human enhancement program at Kendrick-Voss. Three days ago, she copied the program's files onto a flash drive, tried to get them to a journalist, and was poisoned for it. She checked into Mercy General under a fake name. She has maybe six hours without treatment (Rosa's healing can stabilize her). She has maybe six minutes if Bastion finds her.

If the players reach her, she can tell them:
- *"Kendrick-Voss has been running human trials for eighteen months. Enhancement protocols. They told us it was theoretical. It wasn't."*
- *"The subjects — they recruited them from shelters, halfway houses, immigration holding. People no one would look for."*
- *"Creed — Silas Creed — he runs the field operations. He was one of the first volunteers. Whatever they did to him, it worked. He believes in it."*
- *"I have everything on a drive. Lab reports, subject files, authorization chains. It's in my coat pocket."* (She gestures weakly to a bag under the bed.)
- *"They'll burn it all. The tower, the records, the subjects. By morning, none of it will have existed."*

**The flash drive** is the key evidence. If the players secure it, they have enough to blow the whole program open — but only if they get it to someone who can act on it.

**What this incident reveals:**
- Bastion Group is hunting a specific whistleblower: Dr. Elena Vasik.
- The experiments were real, ongoing, and targeted vulnerable people.
- Silas Creed is the field commander and a product of the program.
- There is physical evidence (the flash drive) that can expose everything.
- The clock is ticking — Bastion's timeline is 90 minutes from blackout to extraction.

## Act 2: Convergence

As the players investigate their respective incidents, the information funnels them toward each other and toward the same conclusions. The GM should let this happen naturally — don't force it, but don't fight it either.

### How the threads connect:

**Marcus → Mercy General or The Convoy.** The encrypted radio and the name "Mercy General" give him a destination. If he decrypted the chatter, he also knows about Warehouse 11.

**Dee → Mercy General or Ridgeline.** She has sedated test subjects and a manifest pointing to WH-11-BRN. If she wants to understand what she's looking at, she needs a scientist. Ridgeline University is lit up across the city. Or she follows the Bastion radio chatter to Mercy General.

**Jordan and Rosa → Already together.** They're in the same building. The siege forces cooperation. Once they find Dr. Vasik, they have the most complete picture of anyone.

**Samir → Mercy General.** Dr. Vasik's voicemail sends him directly to the hospital. He arrives mid-siege and has to figure out how to get in.

### The meeting

The most natural convergence point is **Mercy General.** Three of five characters are there or heading there. The GM should play the meeting as tense — these are strangers in a crisis, not a team. They have different information and different reasons for being here. The first interaction should have friction:

- Marcus is a security guard who just watched his building explode and fought armed men. He's suspicious of everyone.
- Dee is a professional thief who was there to steal from the same convoy Bastion hit. She has reasons to be cagey about her motives.
- Jordan is a college student whose powers just went live in a hostage situation. They're scared.
- Samir is an academic who just drove across a blacked-out city to find his dying mentor. He's desperate.
- Rosa is an EMT who just discovered she can heal people with her hands. She doesn't have time for introductions — she has a patient.

**Let the players roleplay this.** The tension of strangers forced together is the emotional core of the adventure. Don't skip it.

### Putting it together

Once the players share information — willingly or reluctantly — the picture becomes clear:

1. Kendrick-Voss Biotech was running illegal human enhancement experiments (Division 9).
2. Bastion Group is systematically destroying the evidence: the lab (tower), the subjects (convoy), and the whistleblower (Vasik).
3. Silas Creed is commanding the operation, and he's headed to Warehouse 11 in The Barrens for helicopter extraction.
4. They have approximately 40 minutes before the blackout lifts and Bastion disappears.
5. The flash drive in Vasik's possession is the only surviving proof.

**The choice:** The players need to get to The Barrens and stop the extraction. But they also need to protect Dr. Vasik, secure the flash drive, and deal with whatever's left of the three incidents (civilians at the hospital, test subjects in the convoy, a burning building in the Spire District).

This is where the sandbox pays off. Let the players decide their priorities. There is no single correct answer.

## Act 3: The Barrens

### Warehouse 11

A decommissioned cold storage facility on the Paramount City waterfront. Three stories of corrugated steel and concrete, loading docks facing the water, a flat roof big enough for a helicopter landing. Bastion has been using it as a staging area for a week — it's not a permanent base, just a waypoint.

### The Layout

**Ground Floor:** Open warehouse space, partially filled with shipping containers (some converted to holding cells for test subjects). A makeshift command post in the former office area — radios, laptops, maps of the city with the three incident locations marked. Two cargo vans parked inside, engines running.

**Second Floor:** Catwalks and storage. Provides elevated firing positions overlooking the ground floor. A single staircase and a freight elevator (currently unpowered).

**Roof:** Flat, reinforced. A landing zone is marked with infrared strobes (visible with darkvision / Low-Light Awareness). The extraction helicopter is 15 minutes out when the players arrive.

**Loading Dock:** Faces the water. A boat is tied up — Bastion's backup extraction if the helicopter is compromised.

### The Opposition

**Silas Creed (Ironhand)** is here, overseeing final preparations. He is calm, efficient, and ready for trouble — but not specifically expecting the players.

**Bastion Operators (6-8)** are present, split between the ground floor (loading cargo) and the roof (prepping the LZ). They are alert but not in combat posture — they believe the three diversionary operations bought them clean time.

**The Test Subjects:** If Bastion successfully recovered the convoy cargo, 4 sedated subjects are in the shipping container cells. They are alive but in bad shape. One of them — **Subject 17** — is semi-conscious and terrified.

### Infiltration

The Barrens are dark — Sector 4, no power. The waterfront is deserted. The players can approach from multiple directions:

- **South (street approach):** A chain-link fence and 100 yards of open lot. Exposed, but the fastest route. A single Bastion lookout with night vision watches this approach.
- **East (waterfront):** Along the docks. More cover (other warehouses, rusted containers) but the footing is treacherous. No lookout, but the loading dock is lit with battery-powered floodlights.
- **West (adjacent warehouse roof):** If the players are creative, they can access the roof of Warehouse 10 (abandoned, unlocked) and cross to Warehouse 11's roof. This puts them above the Bastion operators on the LZ, but the jump between buildings is 12 feet (DC 12 Strength/Athletics, DC 14 Dexterity/Acrobatics).
- **Below (drainage tunnels):** A maintenance tunnel runs under the waterfront. It's flooded with a foot of water and smells like death, but it connects to Warehouse 11's subbasement. Dee or Samir might think of this with a DC 14 Intelligence/Investigation check or if they examine the city infrastructure maps at the command post.

### The Confrontation

However the players get in, the climax should be **messy, dynamic, and layered.** This isn't a clean boss fight — it's a chaotic operation with multiple objectives happening simultaneously:

**Objectives the players might pursue:**
1. Stop the helicopter extraction (disable the LZ, signal the pilot off, shoot it down — though that last one has consequences)
2. Confront Silas Creed
3. Free the test subjects
4. Secure or destroy the remaining evidence Bastion is trying to extract
5. Get out alive

**Creed's response:** When he realizes the operation is compromised, Creed does not panic. He makes a tactical decision:

- If the players are clearly outmatched: he offers them a walk-away. *"You've seen too much, but I don't need more bodies tonight. Walk away. You never saw any of this. I'll make sure of it."* He means it — but he'll also hunt them later if they have the flash drive.
- If the players are a real threat: he orders his operators to hold the extraction and engages the most dangerous-looking player personally. He fights smart — uses cover, takes hostages if available, and tries to end it fast.
- If the players are winning: he grabs the highest-value item (evidence drives, a test subject, whatever he can carry) and heads for the boat. The helicopter can be sacrificed. The cargo cannot.

**The helicopter** arrives 15 minutes after the players do (GM can adjust for pacing). It's a civilian model, contracted by Bastion, pilot is not a combatant. If the players disable the LZ (destroy the strobes, block the roof), it will circle and eventually leave. If they signal the pilot that the LZ is hostile (a flare, a visible fight on the roof), the pilot aborts. If the helicopter lands, Creed has 2 rounds to load and go before liftoff.

### The Creed Fight

Silas Creed is the adventure's final challenge. He's not a supervillain — he's a soldier who happens to be very, very good at his job and has been enhanced beyond normal human limits.

**Creed fights like a professional:**
- He uses cover and concealment. He doesn't stand in the open.
- He targets the healer first (Rosa) and the most obvious power threat second (Jordan).
- His iron hand can grapple and crush — if he grabs someone, he squeezes. The sound is bad.
- He will use a test subject or civilian as a human shield if it's tactically advantageous. He doesn't enjoy it. He just does it.
- He retreats when the mission is compromised beyond recovery. He does not fight to the death unless cornered.

**If Creed escapes:** He becomes a recurring antagonist. He knows their faces. He'll be back.

**If Creed is captured:** He says nothing. Not because he's loyal to Bastion, but because he knows the people above Bastion are more dangerous than anything the players can do to him. He'll sit in a cell and wait.

**If Creed is killed:** The players find a Bastion Group contractor ID, a phone with encrypted contacts (uncrackable without serious resources), and a set of dog tags from his military service. The dog tags have a second set of tags welded to them — a subject number: **S-01.**

He was the first.

## Aftermath

### Immediate

- The blackout lifts approximately 90 minutes after it began. Power returns in waves — Sector 3 first, then 2, then 4. The city comes back to life, blinking and confused.
- Emergency services flood the incident sites. PCPD (Paramount City Police Department) establishes perimeters at all three locations.
- If the players secured Dr. Vasik and the flash drive, they have a decision: who do they give the evidence to? The police? A journalist? A federal agency? Do they trust any of them?
- If the test subjects were rescued, they need medical attention — specialized medical attention. Ridgeline University's biomedical department is the best option in the city, and Samir has access.
- The Kendrick-Voss building fire is contained by morning. The company issues a statement blaming a gas main rupture. The stock drops 4%.

### The Morning After

The players wake up (if they sleep) to a city that has already started forgetting. The news cycle is about the blackout — infrastructure failure, grid vulnerability, the mayor promises an investigation. The tower explosion gets 30 seconds. The convoy ambush is reported as a traffic accident. The hospital siege is described as "a disturbance." Bastion's cleanup is working.

Unless the players act.

### Expansion Hooks (Sessions 2-3)

**Who hired Bastion?** Bastion Group is a contractor. They don't run programs — they service them. Someone inside Kendrick-Voss (or above Kendrick-Voss) authorized Division 9, funded the experiments, and called Bastion in to clean up when Vasik threatened to talk. Following the money and the authorization chain leads up — into corporate boardrooms, government contracts, and names that are much bigger than a biotech firm in Paramount City.

**The Test Subjects.** If rescued, the subjects are traumatized, disoriented, and unstable. Their enhancements are inconsistent — some are powerful, some are breaking down, some are experiencing side effects that will kill them without treatment. They need help. They also need to stay hidden, because whoever ran Division 9 will want them back.

**Creed's Network.** If Creed escaped, he's regrouping. He has resources, contacts, and a grudge. He also has a complicated relationship with the truth — he believes in the enhancement program, but he also knows what it cost the subjects. A GM who wants moral complexity can make Creed a villain who occasionally does the right thing for the wrong reasons.

**The Flash Drive.** The data on Vasik's drive is explosive — but it's also incomplete. It names the Paramount City operation but references "parallel programs" in other cities. Division 9 wasn't the only one. There are others. And they're still running.

**The Players Are Known.** Bastion operators saw their faces. Security cameras (the ones that survived) recorded them at the incident sites. The hired guns at Mercy General can identify them in a lineup. The players didn't just stop an operation — they made enemies. The question is whether they also made allies.

## Key NPCs

### Silas Creed — "Ironhand"

**Role:** Primary antagonist. Bastion Group field commander.

**Background:** Ex-military. Special operations, three tours, decorated. Dishonorably discharged after an incident that's still classified — the official story is disobeying orders, but the truth is he followed orders that shouldn't have been given and took the fall for someone above him. Bastion recruited him six months later. He's been their top field asset for five years.

Eighteen months ago, he volunteered for Division 9's enhancement protocol. It worked. Enhanced strength, accelerated healing, pain suppression. The cybernetic left hand was a bonus — Bastion R&D built it to test human-machine integration. It's grafted to his nervous system. He can't take it off.

**Personality:** Calm. Professional. Speaks in short, direct sentences. Doesn't raise his voice. Doesn't bluff. If he says he'll kill you, he means it. If he says he'll let you walk, he means that too. He has a code — it's just not the same as yours.

He believes the enhancement program is necessary. The world is full of powered individuals who got their abilities by accident — genetics, mutation, divine lottery. Bastion's program is the first attempt to create them deliberately, to build something reliable. The cost is ugly. He knows that. He thinks the alternative is worse.

**What he looks like:** Late 40s. 6'1", lean, hard. Short gray hair. A face that looks like it's been broken and set more than once. The left hand is matte black articulated metal from the wrist down — he wears a glove over it in public. He moves like a man who's never been surprised by anything.

**In combat:** He fights with his hands and a tactical knife. The iron hand hits like a mace. He doesn't use guns unless the situation is truly dire — not out of principle, but because he doesn't need them.

---

### Dr. Elena Vasik

**Role:** The whistleblower. The person everyone is trying to find.

**Background:** 58 years old. Biochemist. Spent twenty years at Kendrick-Voss working on genetic research — legitimate, published, respected. When Division 9 started, she was recruited internally. She was told it was theoretical modeling. By the time she realized it wasn't, she was already complicit.

She spent six months documenting everything. Then she tried to get the data to Mara Chin, an investigative journalist at the Paramount City Register. Two days later, she was poisoned. Ricin derivative — slow-acting, designed to look like organ failure. She realized what was happening, grabbed her go-bag and the flash drive, and checked into Mercy General under a fake name.

**Personality:** Exhausted. Terrified. Guilty. She knows she helped build this. She knows "I didn't know" isn't good enough. She's trying to do the right thing now, and she's aware that it might be too late — for the subjects, and for her.

She is not a fighter. She is a scientist who made bad choices and is trying to fix them before she dies.

**What she looks like:** Thin, gray-haired, dark circles under her eyes. Hospital gown. She speaks in a low, precise voice and doesn't waste words. She has the flash drive in a coat pocket in a bag under her hospital bed.

---

### Terrence Sharpe

**Role:** Leader of the hired guns at Mercy General. Secondary antagonist.

**Background:** Career criminal from Paramount City's west side. Armed robbery, assault, one manslaughter charge that was pleaded down. He's not stupid — he's just made a lot of bad decisions. This is the biggest: he was hired through a cutout to lock down a hospital and find a specific patient. He was told it was a "private medical extraction." The money was $200,000 split twelve ways. He didn't ask questions.

**Personality:** Loud, aggressive, but not evil. He's performing toughness because he's terrified. He's never done anything this big and he knows it. If the players confront him with the reality of what he's involved in — human experimentation, corporate murder — he might fold. Or he might double down because he's already in too deep.

## GM Notes

### Pacing

**One-shot (3-4 hours):**
- Act 1 (The Three Incidents): 60-90 minutes. Run each incident as a quick scene — describe the chaos, let each player react, establish the stakes. Don't let any single incident run longer than 20 minutes before cutting to another player.
- Act 2 (Convergence): 30-45 minutes. The players meet, share information, argue, and plan. This is the character development section — don't rush it.
- Act 3 (The Barrens): 60-90 minutes. Infiltration, the Creed confrontation, and the aftermath.

**Extended (2-3 sessions):**
- Session 1: Acts 1 and 2. End on the decision to go to The Barrens.
- Session 2: Act 3 and immediate aftermath.
- Session 3: The morning after. The players decide what to do with the evidence, deal with fallout, and encounter the first signs that Bastion (or whoever hired them) isn't done.

### Difficulty

The adventure is designed for Level 1-3 characters. Combat encounters are scaled accordingly:

- **Hired Guns (Mercy General):** Easy to medium. Groups of 2-3 are manageable for a single character. The full group of 12 is deadly — the players should use stealth, tactics, and the environment, not brute force.
- **Bastion Operators (Tower, Convoy, Barrens):** Medium to hard. They fight tactically and use Tactical Coordination. In groups of 4+, they are a serious threat.
- **Silas Creed:** Hard to deadly. At Level 3, the full party should be able to take him in a straight fight, but it'll cost them. Creed with supporting operators is a deadly encounter. He's designed to be beatable but dangerous — the players should feel like they earned it.

If the party is only 3 players, reduce Bastion operators by 2 in each encounter and remove one of Creed's Legendary Actions.

### Tone Notes

- **Violence has consequences.** People who get shot bleed. Buildings that explode have people in them. When the players fight the hired guns at Mercy General, there are civilians in the next room. This isn't gratuitous — it's grounded. The stakes are real because the consequences are real.
- **Humor is earned.** Dee is funny. Jordan is funny when they're nervous. Let it happen naturally. Don't force jokes, but don't suppress them either. The best comic book teams are funny between the crises.
- **The villain is human.** Creed is not a cartoon. He's a man who made choices and believes in them. He's wrong, but he's coherent. When he explains why the enhancement program matters, he should make just enough sense that the players hesitate — for one second — before deciding he's full of it.
- **The players are not a team yet.** At the start of this adventure, these people are strangers. By the end, they're something else. Don't skip the journey. The moments where they disagree, distrust each other, and then choose to show up anyway — that's the story.

### What If...

**What if the players don't go to The Barrens?**
They might decide to hunker down, protect Vasik, and wait for the police. That's valid. If they do, Creed completes the extraction unchallenged — but the flash drive still exists, and the story continues as a slower burn: the players have to figure out who to trust with the evidence while Bastion hunts them.

**What if a player dies?**
At levels 1-3, death is real. If a player goes down and fails death saves, treat it as a consequence, not a failure. The dead character becomes a martyring moment — the team's first loss. If it happens early (Act 1), the GM should have a backup: one of the test subjects regains consciousness and can be played as a replacement character (use any premade stat block with modified background).

**What if the players side with Creed?**
Unlikely but possible — especially Dee, who might see the pragmatic argument for the enhancement program. If this happens, congratulations: your campaign just got a lot more interesting. The players become assets of Bastion, with all the moral weight that carries. This is a valid story. Let it play out.

**What if the players split up for the entire adventure?**
The adventure is designed to converge, but if the players insist on staying separate, run each thread to its natural conclusion. Marcus ends up at The Barrens alone (bad odds). Dee vanishes with evidence (her default). Jordan and Rosa protect the hospital but never learn the bigger picture. Samir finds Vasik but can't protect her alone. The lesson: they needed each other. That's the point.
