# OP TTRPG — Feats

> Feats in OP are the primary progression lever for player customization. Players choose feats often and they should feel powerful. The system is designed to expand with a large library of custom feats over time — Origin-specific feats, Home Town feats, and general feats — but the foundation starts here with reflavored SRD 5.2 feats.
>
> **Feat Progression:**
> - **Character Creation (Level 0):** 2 Origin Feats. These represent who you were before your Origin Story — human-scale talents, training, and instincts.
> - **Levels 1–12:** 3 additional feats (half-feats only, no Ability Score Improvement as a standalone choice). Timing TBD — likely at levels 1, 5, and 9 or similar pacing.
>
> **Home Town Feats:** Each Home Town also grants one Origin Feat. This is in addition to the 2 chosen at creation.

---

## Feat Categories

### Origin Feats

Available at character creation. No level prerequisite. These are human-scale abilities — things a capable person could have before gaining powers.

### General Feats

Available at level 4+. These represent growth and specialization that comes with experience. In OP, these are the pool for the 3 half-feat picks across levels 1–12 (level prerequisite may be adjusted to fit OP's progression).

### Fighting Style Feats

Available only to Origin Stories that grant the Fighting Style feature (The Soldier, The Hunter, The Oathsworn). These represent specific combat training disciplines.

## Origin Feats

### Alert

*Origin Feat*

You've trained yourself to react before anyone else in the room. Whether it's street instincts, military drilling, or just paranoia that pays off — you're never caught flat-footed.

- **Initiative Proficiency.** When you roll Initiative, you can add your Proficiency Bonus to the roll.
- **Initiative Swap.** Immediately after you roll Initiative, you can swap your Initiative with one willing ally in the same combat. You can't make this swap if you or the ally has the Incapacitated condition.

**OP Note:** Granted by The Industrial City Home Town. A natural pick for Scoundrels, Hunters, and Soldiers — anyone whose survival depends on acting first.

---

### Magic Initiate

*Origin Feat*

You have access to abilities that most people can't explain. Maybe you were born with a spark. Maybe something awakened in you. Maybe you studied something you shouldn't have. Whatever the source, you can do things that aren't normal.

- **Two Cantrips.** You learn two cantrips of your choice from the Cleric, Druid, or Wizard spell list. Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for this feat's spells (choose when you select this feat).
- **Level 1 Spell.** Choose a level 1 spell from the same list you selected for this feat's cantrips. You always have that spell prepared. You can cast it once without a spell slot, and you regain the ability to cast it in that way when you finish a Long Rest. You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have.
- **Spell Change.** Whenever you gain a new level, you can replace one of the spells you chose for this feat with a different spell of the same level from the chosen spell list.

**Repeatable.** You can take this feat more than once, but you must choose a different spell list each time.

**OP Note:** Granted by The Small Town (Cleric list) and The Metropolis (Wizard list). "Spells" in OP should be reflavored per Origin Story — a Genius's Magic Initiate (Wizard) represents gadgets or scientific breakthroughs, not arcane study. A Chosen's Magic Initiate (Cleric) represents channeled power from an unknown source.

---

### Savage Attacker

*Origin Feat*

You hit hard. Harder than you should. Whether it's raw physical power, trained striking technique, or just the kind of meanness that puts extra force behind every swing — when you connect, it counts.

- Once per turn when you hit a target with a weapon, you can roll the weapon's damage dice twice and use either roll against the target.

**OP Note:** Granted by The Capitol Home Town. A natural pick for Bereaved, Soldiers, and Disciples — anyone whose primary contribution is violence.

---

### Skilled

*Origin Feat*

You pick things up fast. Languages, tools, techniques — you've always been the person who could watch someone do something once and then do it yourself. Maybe it's natural aptitude. Maybe you just never had the luxury of specializing.

- You gain proficiency in any combination of three skills or tools of your choice.

**Repeatable.** You can take this feat more than once.

**OP Note:** The flexibility pick. Good for players who want breadth over depth, or who want to fill skill gaps in the party. Pairs naturally with Everymen, Celebrities, and Scoundrels.

---

## General Feats

### Grappler

*General Feat (Prerequisite: Level 4+, Strength or Dexterity 13+)*

You know how to control a fight at close range. Whether it's wrestling training, street fighting instinct, or just being big enough to hold someone down — when you get your hands on someone, the fight changes.

- **Ability Score Increase.** Increase your Strength or Dexterity score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
- **Punch and Grab.** When you hit a creature with an Unarmed Strike as part of the Attack action on your turn, you can use both the Damage and the Grapple option. You can use this benefit only once per turn.
- **Attack Advantage.** You have Advantage on attack rolls against a creature Grappled by you.
- **Fast Wrestler.** You don't have to spend extra movement to move a creature Grappled by you if the creature is your size or smaller.

**OP Note:** The only General half-feat in the SRD 5.2. This is a clear gap — OP needs a much larger library of General half-feats to support the 3 half-feat picks across levels 1–12. Custom feat design is a priority. See Design Notes below.

## Fighting Style Feats

*Available only to Origin Stories with the Fighting Style feature: The Soldier, The Hunter, The Oathsworn.*

### Archery

*Fighting Style Feat (Prerequisite: Fighting Style Feature)*

You're a marksman. Rifles, bows, thrown weapons — anything at range, you put where it needs to go.

- You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with Ranged weapons.

---

### Defense

*Fighting Style Feat (Prerequisite: Fighting Style Feature)*

You know how to use what you're wearing. Armor, padding, a reinforced jacket — you move in it like a second skin, covering gaps others would leave open.

- While you're wearing Light, Medium, or Heavy armor, you gain a +1 bonus to Armor Class.

---

### Great Weapon Fighting

*Fighting Style Feat (Prerequisite: Fighting Style Feature)*

When you swing something big, you swing it like you mean it. Bats, hammers, two-handed blades, heavy pipes — you've learned to put everything into every hit.

- When you roll damage for an attack you make with a Melee weapon that you are holding with two hands, you can treat any 1 or 2 on a damage die as a 3. The weapon must have the Two-Handed or Versatile property to gain this benefit.

---

### Two-Weapon Fighting

*Fighting Style Feat (Prerequisite: Fighting Style Feature)*

Two hands, two weapons, twice the problem. You've trained to fight with a weapon in each hand, and you don't pull punches with either.

- When you make an extra attack as a result of using a weapon that has the Light property, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of that attack if you aren't already adding it to the damage.

## Design Notes

- **The current feat pool is insufficient.** Players pick 5 feats total (2 at creation + 3 across levels 1–12) plus 1 from their Home Town. With only 4 Origin Feats and 1 General half-feat available, the system cannot support meaningful choice yet. Custom feat design is the single highest priority for OP's next development phase.
- **Origin Feats are deliberately human-scale.** Alert is awareness. Skilled is versatility. Savage Attacker is hitting hard. Magic Initiate is the only one that touches the supernatural, and even that can be reflavored as latent talent or early training. These are pre-powers feats — who you were before the Origin Story.
- **Half-feat requirement for level-up feats** means every feat chosen at levels 1–12 must include a +1 ability score increase. This prevents ability score inflation (no standalone ASI picks) while still allowing gradual stat growth. All future custom feats for this tier should follow the half-feat template: +1 to one ability score + one primary benefit + one minor/ribbon benefit.
- **Fighting Style Feats are class-gated.** Only The Soldier, The Hunter, and The Oathsworn have the Fighting Style feature. This is intentional — these are the trained combatants. Other Origin Stories get their combat power from other sources.
- **Ability Score Improvement is excluded as a standalone pick.** Players cannot take ASI as one of their 3 level-up feats. Ability score growth comes exclusively through half-feats. This keeps feat selection interesting and prevents the "I just take +2 STR" non-choice.
- **Epic Boons are cut.** OP caps at level 12. Epic Boons (level 19+ prerequisite) have no place in this system.

### Priority Custom Feat Categories (Future Development)

The following feat categories need to be designed to make the system functional:

1. **General Half-Feats (Level 4+).** The bread and butter. Combat feats, utility feats, skill feats — all following the half-feat template. Target: 15–20 feats minimum to provide real choice.
2. **Origin Story Feats.** Feats locked to specific Origin Stories. A Bereaved-only feat, a Mutant-only feat, etc. These make each Origin feel mechanically distinct beyond class features. Target: 2–3 per Origin Story.
3. **Home Town Feats.** Feats locked to specific Home Towns. Growing up in The Industrial City teaches you things The Capitol doesn't. Target: 1–2 per Home Town.
4. **Power Feats (Tiered).** Feats that represent superhuman abilities — flight, super strength, elemental powers, telepathy. These should be tiered (street-level, city-level, cosmic-level) to maintain a sense of escalation. Target: 10–15 across tiers.

The full feat list should eventually reach 100+ options. Players should never see the full list — they see only what's available to them based on their Origin Story, Home Town, and level. The backend is massive; the player-facing experience is curated.
