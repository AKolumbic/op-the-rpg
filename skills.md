# OP TTRPG — Skill List

> Adapted from the SRD 5.2 skill list for a 20th century alternate-USA superhero setting.
> No 21st century technology exists in this world — no internet, no smartphones, no social media.

---

## Strength

### Athletics
Physical exertion — climbing, swimming, jumping, lifting, grappling. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

---

## Dexterity

### Acrobatics
Balance, tumbling, aerial maneuvers, dodging through obstacles. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Sleight of Hand
Pickpocketing, planting objects, concealing items on your person, lockpicking. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Stealth
Moving unseen and unheard, tailing targets, blending into crowds. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

---

## Intelligence

### History
Knowledge of past events, wars, political movements, historical figures, and the alternate-USA timeline. Grounded in documented, factual events. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Investigation
Deductive reasoning, searching for clues, connecting evidence, analyzing crime scenes. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Lore
*Replaces Religion.* Knowledge of myths, legends, folklore, occult traditions, supernatural phenomena, and ancient or esoteric knowledge that exists outside the historical record. Where History deals in facts, Lore deals in stories — the kind that might turn out to be true.

### Science
*Replaces Arcana.* Knowledge of physics, chemistry, biology, and their applications. Used to understand experimental technology, identify chemical compounds, analyze mutations, or comprehend the theoretical basis behind superpowers.

### Technology
*New skill.* Knowledge of mechanical and electrical systems — radios, engines, early computers, surveillance equipment, weapons systems, industrial machinery. Used to operate, repair, jury-rig, or sabotage 20th century technology.

---

## Wisdom

### Intuition
*Replaces Insight.* Reading people and situations — detecting lies, gauging intentions, understanding motives, sensing when something's off about a room, a plan, or a person. Broader than 5e's Insight; in OP, Intuition is the gut-sense skill that covers both interpersonal reads and situational awareness.

### Medicine
First aid, diagnosing injuries and illness, stabilizing the dying, understanding anatomy. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Perception
Noticing details — spotting a hidden figure, hearing a distant conversation, catching something out of the corner of your eye. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Urban
*Replaces Animal Handling and absorbs urban elements of Survival.* Knowledge of how cities work at ground level — navigating neighborhoods, knowing who runs what territory, finding black markets, reading the social dynamics of a block, identifying undercover cops or gang affiliations. Also covers operating in disaster zones, navigating collapsed infrastructure, and surviving in areas devastated by super-powered conflict within city environments.

### Wilderness
*Replaces Nature and absorbs outdoor elements of Survival.* Tracking, foraging, navigating rural and wild areas, enduring harsh outdoor conditions, identifying plants and animals, reading weather patterns. Covers everything outside city limits — forests, mountains, coastlines, deserts, and any territory where civilization thins out.

---

## Charisma

### Deception
Lying convincingly, disguising yourself, forging documents, creating alibis. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Intimidation
Coercing through threats, physical presence, reputation, or demonstrated power. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

### Performance
Entertaining, public speaking, acting, maintaining a public persona or secret identity. Recontextualized — in OP, Performance also covers managing your hero (or villain) persona in the media: press conferences, radio interviews, public appearances.

### Persuasion
Influencing through charm, logic, or good faith negotiation. Unchanged from SRD 5.2.

## Design Notes

- **Arcana → Science:** Superpowers in OP may have scientific explanations. Science covers the theoretical side — what's happening and why. Technology covers the practical side — how to build, fix, or break things.
- **Religion → Lore:** Religious and occult knowledge is reframed as Lore — myths, legends, folklore, and esoteric knowledge. This creates a clean split with History: History is factual and documented, Lore is everything that might be true but lives outside the historical record. Essential for mystical Origin Stories.
- **Animal Handling → Urban:** Animal Handling is too niche for the genre. Urban fills a gap that 5e has always had — there's no skill for "I grew up in this city and I know how it works." Essential for a superhero game built around Home Towns. Also absorbs the urban/disaster-zone elements of the old Survival skill.
- **Nature → Wilderness:** Nature is reframed and expanded as Wilderness, absorbing the outdoor/tracking elements of the old Survival skill. Covers everything outside city limits.
- **Survival removed as standalone:** Its use cases are fully covered by Urban (city survival) and Wilderness (outdoor survival). No mechanical loss.
- **Skill count at 18:** Parity with 5e maintained. Proficiency math and "choose X skills" from Origins needs no recalibration.
