# OP TTRPG — Home Towns

> Home Towns replace backgrounds from the SRD 5.2. They represent where your character grew up — the place that shaped them before their Origin Story began. Each Home Town maps mechanically to an SRD 5.2 background under the hood, but the player-facing identity is a place, not a profession.
>
> The setting is an unnamed country. Home Towns are archetypes, not specific named cities. This allows GMs and players to define the details — names, geography, culture — for their own version of the world.

---

## Parts of a Home Town

Each Home Town provides the following:

- **Ability Scores.** A Home Town lists three ability scores. Increase one by 2 and another by 1, or increase all three by 1. None can exceed 20.
- **Feat.** A Home Town grants one Origin Feat.
- **Skill Proficiencies.** Two skills reflecting what growing up in this place teaches you.
- **Tool Proficiency.** One tool proficiency.
- **Equipment.** A choice between a starting equipment package and 50 GP.

## The Small Town

*Mapped from: SRD 5.2 Acolyte*

A quiet place where everybody knows everybody. The church, the temple, the local gathering hall — that's the center of life here. Traditions run deep. Faith isn't questioned, it's assumed. People are kind, but they talk, and anyone who's different stands out immediately. If something strange chose you, this is the kind of place where it felt like a miracle or a curse, depending on who you asked.

The Small Town produces people of faith, people of conviction, and people who couldn't wait to leave.

- **Ability Scores:** Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma
- **Feat:** Magic Initiate (Cleric) *(Reflavor: the gift of faith, inherited spiritual sensitivity, or an unexplained connection to something beyond the ordinary — manifesting as minor abilities the townfolk either revered or feared.)*
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Intuition and Lore *(mapped from Insight and Religion)*
- **Tool Proficiency:** Calligrapher's Supplies *(Reflavor: hand-lettering, signmaking, record-keeping — small town skills.)*
- **Equipment:** Choose A or B: (A) Calligrapher's Supplies, Book (family bible, local history, or prayer book), Holy Symbol (or personal keepsake), Parchment (10 sheets), Simple Clothes, 8 GP; or (B) 50 GP

**What The Small Town Teaches You:** Patience. How to read people. That community is everything — and that it can also suffocate you. You know how to listen, how to keep faith (or fake it), and how to spot when someone's hiding something behind a polite smile.

## The Industrial City

*Mapped from: SRD 5.2 Criminal*

Smokestacks, rail yards, crumbling factories, and neighborhoods where the streetlights don't work. The Industrial City was built on labor — steel, coal, manufacturing — and when the jobs dried up, what was left was poverty, crime, and people doing whatever it takes to survive. The police are either absent or corrupt. The gangs run the blocks. Everyone knows someone who's inside, and everyone's got a hustle.

The Industrial City produces survivors, criminals, and people who learned very young that no one is coming to help.

- **Ability Scores:** Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence
- **Feat:** Alert *(Reflavor: you learned to watch your back before you learned to read. Situational awareness is a survival skill here.)*
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Sleight of Hand and Stealth
- **Tool Proficiency:** Thieves' Tools *(Reflavor: lockpicks, hotwiring kits, break-in tools — standard equipment for getting by.)*
- **Equipment:** Choose A or B: (A) 2 Daggers (or knives), Thieves' Tools, Crowbar, 2 Pouches, Street Clothes, 16 GP; or (B) 50 GP

**What The Industrial City Teaches You:** How to move without being seen. How to take what you need. That trust is earned, not given, and that the law is just another system people use to control you. You know the streets — which alleys are dead ends, which cops are dirty, and which doors are never really locked.

## The Metropolis

*Mapped from: SRD 5.2 Sage*

The biggest city in the country. Skyscrapers, universities, museums, financial districts, media headquarters, and more people than you can count. The Metropolis is where culture is made, where money flows, where ideas are born and argued over in lecture halls and newspaper columns. It's exciting, overwhelming, and indifferent — the city doesn't care about you, but if you're smart enough, it'll give you everything.

The Metropolis produces scholars, researchers, journalists, analysts, and anyone whose weapon is knowledge.

- **Ability Scores:** Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom
- **Feat:** Magic Initiate (Wizard) *(Reflavor: academic training, research breakthroughs, or access to institutions that unlocked abilities others don't have. The Metropolis has universities and labs where the cutting edge of science bleeds into the unexplainable.)*
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Science and History *(mapped from Arcana and History)*
- **Tool Proficiency:** Calligrapher's Supplies *(Reflavor: academic writing, technical drafting, grant proposals — the Metropolis runs on paperwork.)*
- **Equipment:** Choose A or B: (A) Walking Stick (quarterstaff), Calligrapher's Supplies, Book (history, science, or philosophy), Parchment (8 sheets), Professional Clothes, 8 GP; or (B) 50 GP

**What The Metropolis Teaches You:** How to think. How to research. That knowledge is power, and that the answers are out there if you know where to look. You know how to navigate institutions — libraries, universities, bureaucracies, corporate offices — and how to find information that other people can't.

## The Capitol

*Mapped from: SRD 5.2 Soldier*

The seat of government. Military bases, intelligence agencies, embassies, monuments, and an entire population that exists to serve the state — or to profit from it. The Capitol is clean, orderly, and controlled. Everything here is about power: who has it, who wants it, and what they're willing to do to keep it. The military presence is visible. The political machinery is not.

The Capitol produces soldiers, officers, agents, bureaucrats, and anyone who was forged in the machinery of the state.

- **Ability Scores:** Strength, Dexterity, Constitution
- **Feat:** Savage Attacker *(Reflavor: military combat training — drilled into you until it became instinct.)*
- **Skill Proficiencies:** Athletics and Intimidation
- **Tool Proficiency:** Choose one Gaming Set *(Reflavor: cards, dice, chess — soldiers pass the time somehow. Gaming is how bonds are formed in barracks and break rooms.)*
- **Equipment:** Choose A or B: (A) Spear (or baton), Shortbow (or sidearm), 20 Arrows (or ammunition), Gaming Set (same as above), Healer's Kit, Quiver (or holster), Duty Clothes, 14 GP; or (B) 50 GP

**What The Capitol Teaches You:** Discipline. Chain of command. That the world runs on orders, and the people who give them aren't always right — but the people who follow them stay alive. You know how to fight, how to follow (or give) orders, and how to survive the grind of institutional life without losing yourself.

## Design Notes

- **Home Towns are archetypes, not proper nouns.** "The Industrial City" is not a specific city — it's a type of place. This lets every table define their own version. One group's Industrial City might be a rust belt hellhole; another's might be a port city with a shipyard economy. The archetype provides the mechanical and narrative framework; the specifics are up to the group.
- **The country is unnamed.** This is deliberate. The setting should feel like it could be America, could be somewhere else, could be an alternate reality. Keeping it vague preserves flexibility and avoids real-world political baggage.
- **Skill proficiencies have been updated to OP's skill list.** Insight → Intuition, Religion → Lore, Arcana → Science. These map to the same mechanical function but use OP terminology.
- **Feat reflavoring is suggested, not mandatory.** Magic Initiate (Cleric) from The Small Town could be literal divine-touched abilities or could be folk traditions that border on the supernatural. The player decides.
- **Equipment has been lightly reflavored** to fit a 20th century setting (knives instead of daggers, street clothes instead of traveler's clothes) while keeping the mechanical values identical.
- **Four Home Towns is a starting set.** The system is designed to expand. Future Home Towns could include: The Harbor (port city, trade, sailors), The Frontier (edge of civilization, self-reliance), The Estate (old money, private schools, family legacy), The Underground (subculture, counterculture, hidden communities), and more. Each new Home Town is a new background equivalent with its own skill proficiencies, feat, and lore.
- **Home Town-specific feats are now available.** Each Home Town has 2 custom feats in addition to its granted Origin Feat. At character creation, a player can choose the Home Town's default Origin Feat OR one of its Home Town-specific feats. See feats.md for the full list.
